# Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla (1856–1943) was an inventor and electrical engineer born in Smiljan, in present-day Croatia, when this territory belonged to the Austro-Hungarian Empire.

From a very young age, he stood out for his photographic memory and his ability to visualize machines completely in his mind before building them. He studied electrical engineering in Graz and philosophy in Prague, although he did not complete his studies. Even so, his technical knowledge and creativity led him to work in various European companies before emigrating to the United States in 1884.

![Nikola Tesla](/nikola-tesla.jpg)

He worked briefly in New York for Thomas Edison, but their opposing approaches to electricity led them to separate. Edison defended direct current, while Tesla promoted alternating current, a system he considered more efficient for transmitting energy over long distances.

This difference led to the so-called **"War of the Currents"**, in which Tesla, together with George Westinghouse, demonstrated the advantages of alternating current and succeeded in making it the global standard.

Tesla also developed inventions such as the induction motor, the Tesla coil, and various radio and wireless control devices. His ideas were visionary, and many were decades ahead of their time.

Although he faced financial difficulties in his later years, he is now recognized as one of the most brilliant and influential inventors in history.
